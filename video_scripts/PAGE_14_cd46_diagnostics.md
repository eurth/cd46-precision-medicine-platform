For CD46, the theranostic question is: can you image CD46 expression before
treatment to confirm target positivity? This matters because enrolment in
a CD46 RLT trial should be restricted to CD46-high patients — confirmed
not just by IHC on a biopsy collected months ago, but ideally by a whole-body molecular imaging scan that shows real-time target distribution.

This page brings together the normal-tissue safety footprint, the germline context,

## SCREEN 1 — GTEx Normal Tissue Panel (75 seconds)

[DO: Point to the GTEx normal tissue expression chart — bar chart of CD46 mRNA
     across 54 normal tissue types, sorted from highest to lowest.]

[SAY:]
"GTEx — the Genotype-Tissue Expression project. 54 human tissue types,
17,382 samples from post-mortem donors with no known major illness.
This is the normal baseline expression profile of CD46.

The highest normal-tissue CD46 mRNA levels appear in: bronchial epithelium,
kidney cortex, salivary gland, and some liver regions. These are the tissues
that carry the highest inherent normal expression and therefore the highest
potential off-target signal in a CD46-targeted imaging or therapy study.

Lowest normal-tissue expression: brain cortex, skeletal muscle, heart — where
CD46 is expressed at very low levels. These tissues present minimal normal
background signal for a PET imaging study and minimal expected off-target
dosimetry for a therapeutic agent.

For your pre-clinical dosimetry planning: the GTEx bar chart is the first
data-driven input into your organ weighting factors for a MIRD-based
dosimetry model. It tells you where to expect background uptake before a
single patient is dosed."

## SCREEN 2 — ClinVar Germline Context (30 seconds)

[DO: Point to the ClinVar section — germline variant table or summary card.]

[SAY:]
"ClinVar shows the germline variant landscape of CD46. Importantly:
no pathogenic germline variants in CD46 are classified as causing
cancer predisposition. The ClinVar entries are primarily benign or
uncertain significance variants in complement deficiency context —
rare immune disorders — not malignancy.

This is relevant for IND toxicology: you are targeting a tumour-overexpressed
surface antigen with no germline cancer predisposition role. That simplifies
the regulatory safety narrative."

## SCREEN 3 — Theranostic PET Agent: YS5 (90 seconds)

[DO: Point to the YS5 section — preclinical imaging data panel, trial info, or reference card.]

[SAY:]
"YS5 is the most advanced CD46 theranostic imaging agent in the literature.
Published by Evans Lab at UCSF — the same group that discovered FOR46 —
YS5 is a fully human anti-CD46 antibody fragment engineered for PET imaging.

In preclinical prostate cancer xenograft models, 89Zr-YS5 PET showed:
high tumour uptake in PSMA-negative, CD46-positive tumours — the exact
patient population FOR46 is targeting therapeutically.
Low kidney and normal tissue background uptake relative to tumour.
Tumour-to-blood ratio sufficient for clear diagnostic contrast at 72 hours post-injection.

This means the theranostic framework already has a proof-of-concept imaging
agent. For a radiopharmaceutical programme: if 89Zr-YS5 confirms CD46
expression in a patient's tumour on PET, that patient is a positive selection
for the companion therapeutic.

The same antibody backbone — or a derivative — could serve as the dosimetry
scan that precedes each 225Ac treatment cycle. This is the same model
as 68Ga-PSMA PET confirming PSMA positivity before lutetium-PSMA-617 therapy."
