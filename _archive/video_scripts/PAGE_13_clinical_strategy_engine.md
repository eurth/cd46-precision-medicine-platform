## STAGE 1 — Target Biology (60 seconds)

[DO: Click or navigate to Stage 1. Point to the evidence summary for Stage 1.]

[SAY:]
"Stage 1: Target Biology. The questions this stage answers:
Is CD46 overexpressed in the target indication? In how many patients?
Is the overexpression prognostically significant? What is the molecular
mechanism driving overexpression?

The platform populates Stage 1 from the Expression Atlas — TCGA mRNA data
across 33 cancer types — and the Survival Outcomes page — 14 statistically
significant prognostic associations.

The Stage 1 summary for a prostate cancer programme:
CD46 is overexpressed in PRAD relative to 30 of 33 other cancer types.
CD46-high patients have a hazard ratio of approximately 1.65 for overall
survival. Mechanism: AR-signalling suppression of CD46 transcription in
primary disease reverses under ARPI treatment, upregulating CD46 in the
castration-resistant setting. Evidence grade: TCGA and published SU2C cohort.
Stage 1 closes. Proceed to Stage 2."

## STAGE 2 — Drug Design Rationale (60 seconds)

[DO: Navigate to Stage 2.]

[SAY:]
"Stage 2: Drug Design Rationale. Questions answered:
What delivery mechanism is biologically optimal? Which isotope?
What is the safety basis — therapeutic index, organs at risk?

The platform populates Stage 2 from the Dosimetry Safety module and
the Drug Pipeline comparative analysis.

Stage 2 summary: alpha particle RLT preferred over beta for mCRPC micrometastatic
disease based on the 2-to-3-cell diameter range matching the CD46 overexpression
gradient. 225Ac at 9.9 days half-life. Tumour H-score 300, renal H-score 200 —
theoretical TI of 1.5 or above. Comparators: FOR46 ADC and BC8 pretargeted RIT
both in active Phase 1 — no direct alpha-RLT comparator registered. Evidence gap
that this programme would address. Stage 2 closes."

## STAGE 3 — Patient Selection (60 seconds)

[DO: Navigate to Stage 3.]

[SAY:]
"Stage 3: Patient Selection. Questions answered:
Which molecular subtype should be enrolled? What is the screening ratio?
What prior therapy requirement defines the enriched population?

The platform populates Stage 3 from the GENIE patient data, the Biomarker Panel
complementarity analysis, and the Patient Eligibility Scorer.

Stage 3 summary: primary enrolment population — PSMA-low, CD46-high mCRPC,
post-ARPI treatment. Molecular enrichment optional: AR-PTEN co-altered patients
by GENIE subtype analysis. Expected eligible fraction: approximately 20-25% of
mCRPC patients screened. Screening ratio estimate: 4:1 per eligible patient.
Biomarker strategy: dual IHC or PSMA-PET scan plus CD46 mRNA or IHC on recent biopsy.
Stage 3 closes."
