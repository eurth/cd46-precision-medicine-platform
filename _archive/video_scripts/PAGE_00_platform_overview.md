## SCREEN 1 — Integrated Data Sources (60 seconds)

[DO: Scroll slowly to the 'Integrated Data Sources' section. Point to each line as you name it.]

[SAY:]
"The six integrated datasets are listed here. Let me name each one with its
key contribution.

TCGA — The Cancer Genome Atlas — our primary source for mRNA expression and
survival analysis. 25 cancer types. Approximately 2,800 patients with
overall survival and progression-free interval data.

The Human Protein Atlas — protein-level IHC expression for CD46 across 81
human tissues, both normal and tumour. This is the protein evidence that
validates mRNA findings at the cell-surface level — where the therapeutic
antibody will actually bind.

DepMap — the Dependency Map project — CRISPR functional screens across
1,186 cancer cell lines. This answers not just 'where is CD46 expressed'
but 'does CD46 matter for cancer cell survival'.

ClinicalTrials.gov — 14 active CD46 clinical trials. Pulled via the live
API at the time of knowledge graph construction.

ChEMBL — the drug-target interaction database. European Bioinformatics
Institute. Provides the pharmacological context for drugs in the pipeline.

And AACR Project GENIE Release 19 — 271,837 sequenced cancer patients
from major academic medical centres worldwide. The largest publicly available
real-world cancer genomics dataset."

[PAUSE — hold on the data source list for 3 seconds]

## SCREEN 2 — Key Findings (75 seconds)

[DO: Scroll to the 'Key Findings' section. Point to each bullet as you name it.]

[SAY:]
"The key findings section is the platform's scientific output in summary form.
These are not editorial opinions — each one is computed from the datasets
you just saw.

Prostate adenocarcinoma, ovarian cancer, and bladder cancer are the top priority
indications. This ranking combines CD46 expression level, protein confirmation,
CRISPR functional dependency, survival impact, and clinical trial activity.
No single metric — all five together.

The PSMA-CD46 complementarity finding. Approximately 35% of metastatic
castration-resistant prostate cancer patients are PSMA-low — they do not
qualify for Pluvicto, the current FDA-approved lutetium-177 therapy. Of those
35%, the majority are CD46-high. 225Ac-CD46 is designed specifically for
this population.

Androgen blockade upregulates CD46. Enzalutamide and abiraterone — which are
standard-of-care treatments for advanced prostate cancer — suppress androgen
receptor signalling, and AR signalling suppresses CD46 transcription. Blocking
AR therefore increases CD46 expression. Patients who progress on standard
therapy are progressively better candidates for CD46-targeted therapy.

Alpha particles from actinium-225 produce 20 times more DNA double-strand breaks
per track compared to beta emitters like lutetium-177. This is the mechanistic
basis for the therapeutic hypothesis."

[PAUSE]
